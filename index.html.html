Medical Passport

Dialysis Continuity Plan 🏥

Critical access profile: DJ has three distinct access points that require careful documentation and coordination with each receiving dialysis center abroad. Each must be disclosed and confirmed before departure.
🔗
Left Groin Catheter
Current primary access point. Tunneled femoral catheter for hemodialysis. Must document: placement date, catheter type and brand, dressing protocol, flow rates achieved, any history of dysfunction or infection. International centers need this to plan your chair placement and nursing care protocol.
Current Active Access
⚠️
Right Arm Fistula (Non-functional)
AVF created but not achieving functional maturation or thrombosed. Document: creation date, original vessel used, reason for non-function (thrombosis, poor flow, failed maturation), any interventions attempted. Receiving nephrologists need this history to assess future access options and avoid redundant procedures.
Non-functional — Documented
🫀
Chest HERO Graft
HeRO (Hemodialysis Reliable Outflow) Graft — catheter-free venous outflow device placed in chest. Document: implant date, outflow vessel used, graft segment location, any revisions, current flow characteristics. This is specialized access — confirm receiving center has experience managing HERO grafts. Not all international centers have seen them. Contact ahead.
HERO Graft — Specialized Access
📋
Complex Access History
Three access points = complex vascular access history. This must be front and center in your medical passport. Your US nephrologist letter must explicitly state all three access sites, their current status, and your standard dialysis parameters (Kt/V target, blood flow rate, session duration, frequency).
Multi-Site Access History
Action checklist: (1) Ask US nephrologist for access-specific letter covering all three sites · (2) Get current imaging (doppler/ultrasound) of right arm fistula and HERO graft — take copies · (3) Contact receiving clinics abroad specifically asking if they manage HERO grafts · (4) Bring physical copies of all access documentation in carry-on · (5) Research target country's interventional nephrology capability before finalizing destination.

Tax Implications — US Expat
FBAR / FATCA
US citizens abroad must file annual FBAR (FinCEN 114) if foreign accounts exceed $10,000. FATCA requires reporting foreign financial assets.
Foreign Earned Income Exclusion
FEIE (Form 2555) lets you exclude up to $126,500 (2024) of foreign earned income from US taxes. Bona fide residence or physical presence test required.
State taxes
Michigan: file a Michigan tax return for your last year. Sever Michigan residency cleanly — update voter registration, driver's license, bank address.
Social Security
US-Germany, US-Canada, US-Netherlands have totalization agreements — avoid double Social Security taxation. Ireland does not have one — check current status.
Tax treaties
US has tax treaties with Germany, Canada, Netherlands, Ireland, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Belgium, Portugal. These prevent double taxation on income.
Pro tip
Hire an expat-specialist CPA for your first year abroad. Cost: $300–600. Saves far more in mistakes. Search: expatriate tax specialist.
Recommended savings target before departure: €15,000–20,000 (or currency equivalent) in liquid savings. This covers: move costs, 3-month emergency reserve, healthcare bridge, first housing deposit, visa fees, and buffer for setup costs. Start saving aggressively from Month 1 of the roadmap.
